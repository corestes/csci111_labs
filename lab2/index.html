Almaty

Almaty is the largest city in Kazakhstan with the population of about 2 million people.
It is located in the foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau at 700-900 m above the sea level.
Almaty has been the capital of Kazakhstan for the most of the 20th century, from 1929 to the fall of the Soviet Union in 1991.

History

  • 1000-900 BC: first settlements established
  • Beginning of XIII century: the city is destroyedby Mongols and subsequently rebuilt
  • End of XVI century: the newly rebuilt settlement falls into decline due to the descrease of the Silk Road trade
  • 1856: Fort Verny established
  • 1887: An earthquake destroes most of Verny
  • 1918: Soviet power esyavlished in Verny
  • 1921: Verny renamed as Alma-ata
  • 1927: Capital of Kazakhstan autonomous Soviet Republic moved to Alma-Ata from Kyzyl-Orda
  • 1936: New plan by Architecture and Planning Bureau to establish rectangular distrits and enchance Almaty as a capital of Kazakh SSR
  • 1941-1944: Numerous industrial facilities and cultural institutions evacuated into the city from the war.
  • 1966: Medeu dam constructed.
  • 1993: Capital moved to Astana
  • 2011: First metro station constructed
